Anatomy of a PCB

The Core Layers

A standard PCB is a composite structure, laminated into a single unit. Each layer plays a vital role in its function and reliability.

  • 1.
    Substrate (FR-4): The base material, typically fiberglass, providing rigidity and insulation.
  • 2.
    Copper: A thin, conductive layer etched to form traces, pads, and planes.
  • 3.
    Solder Mask: A protective layer (usually green) that insulates copper traces from accidental contact.
  • 4.
    Silkscreen: The top layer that adds letters, numbers, and symbols for component identification.
